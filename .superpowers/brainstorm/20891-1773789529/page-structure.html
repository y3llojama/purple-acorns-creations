Page Architecture

How consolidated should the site feel? Three approaches — click to select.

Navigation bar with 6 links
/ HOME — hero image + brief intro + CTAs
/shop — Square embed only
/our-story — text + photos
/gallery — photo grid
/events — calendar list
/contact — form

A — Scattered Multi-Page

Every section lives on its own page. Visitor must click around to discover the brand. Works for large stores but feels thin for a boutique studio. Easy to add pages later.

Pros

  • Clean per-page SEO
  • Shareable deep links

Cons

  • Low first-visit impact
  • Story gets buried

B — Full One-Pager

Everything lives on the homepage as stacked scroll sections. Immersive brand journey. Great for Instagram link-in-bio. The shop section shows featured pieces with a "view all on Square" link.

Pros

  • Immersive, no friction
  • Perfect for mobile
  • Single link in bio

Cons

  • Can feel heavy to scroll
  • Less SEO surface

C — Hybrid ★

Homepage is a rich scroll showcasing everything — story teaser, featured pieces, gallery strip, next event, Instagram. Two dedicated pages: /shop (full Square embed) and /our-story (long-form). Everything else lives on home.

Pros

  • Strong first impression
  • Shop has own clean URL
  • Best of both worlds

Cons

  • Homepage needs good photos
